Document Name: Income Certificate

Details:

An Income Certificate is an official document issued by the government certifying a person's or family's annual income from all sources. It is used to avail various welfare schemes, subsidies, and reservation benefits provided by the central and state governments in India. Income certificates are issued by revenue departments at the taluk or district level.

Eligibility Criteria:
  • Applicant must be a resident of the respective state.
  • Proof of income must be verifiable from legal sources.
  • Only one income certificate per financial year per family is allowed.
  • Applicant must not already possess a valid income certificate for the current year.
Documents Required:
  • Application form
  • Proof of Identity (Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, PAN Card)
  • Proof of Address (Ration Card, Utility Bill, Aadhaar)
  • Salary certificate (for salaried individuals)
  • Income proof like Form 16, IT Returns, or affidavit (for self-employed)
  • Affidavit declaring income from all sources (duly signed and notarized)
  • Passport-size photograph
Benefits:
  • Eligibility for scholarships (like MYSY, NSP, etc.)
  • Access to fee concessions in educational institutions
  • Qualification for EWS (Economically Weaker Section) reservation
  • Eligibility for government welfare schemes and subsidies
  • Required for availing housing or ration-related benefits under government schemes
